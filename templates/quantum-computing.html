Quantum Computing

Summary

Quantum computing uses quantum bits (qubits) to perform complex calculations faster than classical computers. Developed since the 1980s, it's promising for fields like cryptography and drug discovery but faces challenges with error rates and scalability.

Main Content

Principles

Qubits can exist in superposition and entanglement, enabling exponential speedups (e.g., Shor's algorithm for factoring).

  • Perspective 1: Optimists see it revolutionizing AI and medicine (IBM, Google advancements).
  • Perspective 2: Skeptics highlight 'quantum winter' risks due to noise and high costs.

Applications

Optimization, simulation, encryption breaking.
